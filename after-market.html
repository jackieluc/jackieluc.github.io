The After Market of Sneakers

A brief look on the lucrative after market phenomenon.

According to Hypebeast and StockX, the most expensive (resale) sneakers of 2016 were:

Rank Brand Style/Colour Retail Price Resale Price
5. Adidas NMD_R1 PK / "Red Apple" $170 USD $2,327 USD

adidas Originals made these in honour of expanding their brand's ever-growing lifestyle initiatives in New York City. This New York exclusive sneaker has a limited number of 200 pairs that will be up for grabs for $170 USD. Each pair is tagged with a number 1 through 200.
4. Adidas NMD_R1 PK / "Pitch Black" N/A $2,972 USD

adidas Originals debuts its much-anticipated coloured BOOST model on the NMD_R1 PK. An extremely limited edition of 500 "Pitch Black" NMD_R1 PK models will be gifted to special partners and influencers. Each pair is tagged with a number 1 through 500. Only a select few individuals will receive the models in an "Urban Utility" set. Included is a blacked-out Rimowa Topas Multiwheel 45L suitcase, Porter Travel Pouch, (multee)project Type 2L Matte Black carabiner, a SIGG Traveler ALu Black 1L bottle, and an adidas Orginals NMD black sneaker dust bag.
3. Nike HyperAdapt 1.0 $720 USD $3,528 USD

Nike introduces the first and highly anticipated, auto-lacing sneaker, made for the retail market.
2. Pharrell x adidas "Human Race" NMD / "Friends and Family" N/A $7,535 USD

Pharrell's collaboration with adidas Originals on a new NMD silhouette. This version is executed with Chinese characters that signify "Friends" and "Family", one on each shoe. The "Friends and Family" pairs only made it into the hands of close friends and family.
1. Nike MAG N/A $28,638 USD

Nike took 27 years to finally make a real, auto-lacing Nike Mag like Marty McFly wore in Back to the Future Part II, but they were only able to create 100 pairs. The majority were given away in a raffle, with the rest being auctioned.
